FalconLedger Emission Blueprint v2
 My notes on solving the inflation concerns
1. Current Programmed Model (What I Have Now)
From the code I already have (epoch-emission.md), the model works like this:
 Starts at 0.5% of treasury per epoch
 Halves every 208 epochs (~4 years)
 Floors at 0.01% around year 24 and stays there forever
 Treasury shrinks over time (double decay)
 Currently only pays validators — LPs not included yet
Problems I'm seeing:
 Year 1 inflation is ~23-26% — way too aggressive
 Takes until year 12+ before it even gets to reasonable levels
 Permanent 0.01% floor means infinite slow dilution forever
 Hard to explain and probably won't look great to people coming in later
2. What I'm Thinking About Now
Instead of big step changes every 4 years, what if the rate declines smoothly every epoch?
This would feel much cleaner and more professional.
Idea I'm exploring:
 Start at 12%
 Decline gradually every single epoch (very small reduction each time)
 Hit around 3.5% by year 6
 Then keep drifting down toward 1.5% long term
 Total emission still around 196B

4. Benefits of C.I.D Model (Continuous Inflationary Decline)
 Gets to reasonable/good inflation levels much faster (by year 6 instead of year 12+)
 Much smoother decline — no big sudden drops that can feel aggressive
 Long-term rate of 1.5% feels more sustainable than a permanent tiny floor
 Easier and cleaner to explain as a steady, controlled reduction
 Still provides strong early incentives for the first 2–3 years
 Better market perception overall — aligns with how successful chains eventually behaved
 Designed with PoPL in mind from the beginning (LPs can be included cleanly)
These are just my current working thoughts. Nothing is locked in yet.
