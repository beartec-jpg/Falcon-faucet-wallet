1. Executive Summary
This blueprint defines a native protocol-level lending system for FalconLedger, a quantum-resistant fork of
the XRP Ledger. The system introduces open-ended fixed-rate lending pools with over-collateralization,
combined with a powerful Proof of Participation & Liquidity (PoPL) incentive mechanism.
Key Innovations:
 Open-ended fixed-rate lending — Borrowers get predictable rates without hard maturity dates; repay
anytime.
 Native over-collateralized model — Falcon as primary collateral for borrowing FUsd (or other pool
assets).
 PoPL Emissions — Fixed 0.5% network emission split between validators and liquidity providers. LPs
earn Falcon proportionally to their share of supplied liquidity (no oracle dependency for rewards).
 Dual yield for LPs — Organic interest from borrowers + protocol Falcon emissions.
 Reuses existing AMM infrastructure — Share accounting, pool UI patterns, and liquidation paths from
current AMM pools.
The design prioritizes simplicity, auditability, and strong bootstrap incentives for mainnet launch while
maintaining the security and performance characteristics of the underlying ledger.
2. Vision: Proof of Participation & Liquidity (PoPL)
FalconLedger evolves beyond traditional validator-only rewards. In the PoPL model, economic security
(validators) and economic activity (liquidity provision) are both first-class citizens rewarded from a fixed
emission pool.
This creates a flywheel: More liquidity → more borrowing capacity → more organic interest + protocol emissions
→ higher effective APY → more liquidity.
Emission Philosophy
Total network emission remains fixed at 0.5%. The split between validators and LPs is a
governance-configurable parameter. Early in the network's life, a larger share goes to LPs to bootstrap real
usage. Over time, as organic interest volume grows, the LP allocation can be tapered.
3. System Overview
Core Components
 LendingPool — Protocol object representing a supply pool for a specific asset (e.g. FUsd). Tracks total
supplied, borrowed, interest accrued, and LP shares.
 LendingPosition / Shares — Per-user share ownership in a pool. Used for both interest distribution and
PoPL emissions.
 Collateral Tracking — Separate from the pool. Borrowers post Falcon (or supported assets) as collateral
against borrowed FUsd.

4. Core Lending Mechanics
4.1 Supply Side (Liquidity Providers)
Users supply the pool's primary asset (initially FUsd) and receive LendingShares proportional to their deposit.
They earn two revenue streams: (1) interest paid by borrowers, and (2) Falcon emissions from the PoPL
program.
4.2 Borrow Side
Borrowers post collateral (primarily Falcon) and borrow the pool asset (FUsd). The position is
over-collateralized at a configurable ratio (target: 150% minimum). Interest accrues continuously based on
ledger close time using the pool's fixed APR.

4.3 Interest Accrual (Open-Ended)
No hard maturity. Interest is calculated on every relevant transaction (repay, partial repay, liquidation, claim)
using:
Accrued = Principal × (APR / SecondsPerYear) × ΔLedgerCloseTime
This keeps the system simple while giving borrowers flexibility.

5.3 Dual Yield
LPs receive the sum of (a) their pro-rata share of borrower-paid interest and (b) their pro-rata Falcon emissions.
This creates highly competitive effective APY, especially during the bootstrap phase.

8. Liquidation & Risk Management
Liquidation is triggered when a position's Health Factor falls below the configured threshold (typically 1.0–1.1).
Anyone can initiate liquidation. The liquidator repays part or all of the debt and receives collateral at a discount
(bonus). The repaid amount (including interest) flows back to the pool for LPs.
Because collateral (Falcon) and borrowed asset (FUsd) are different, liquidation can optionally route through
the existing AMM for efficient swaps.

10. Implementation Roadmap
 Phase 0 (Current) — Finalize blueprint, tokenomics parameters, and economic model. Community /
validator alignment on PoPL split.
 Phase 1 — Core lending objects + transactions in daemon (LendingPool,
Supply/Borrow/Repay/Withdraw). Testnet deployment.
 Phase 2 — Collateral & health factor engine + basic liquidation. PoPL reward accumulator (share-ratio
only).
 Phase 3 — Client integration in Falcon-faucet-wallet (new lending UI, borrow/repay flows, position
dashboard). Full testnet E2E.
 Phase 4 — Security review, invariant testing, economic simulations. Amendment proposal & validator
voting.
 Phase 5 — Mainnet activation with aggressive LP emissions for launch. Ongoing monitoring + taper
schedule activation.
11. Security Considerations
 All new logic must pass through standard preclaim/apply/invariant checks.
 Health factor and liquidation math must be deterministic and resistant to manipulation.
 Reward distribution must be fair and resistant to flash-liquidity gaming (time-weighting or minimum
participation periods can be added).
 Oracle usage is isolated to collateral pricing only — reward math does not depend on oracles.
 Full security report and audit process (following the pattern already used for AMM/bridge features).
